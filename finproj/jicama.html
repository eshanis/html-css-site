Jicama Taco

Thinly sliced jicama slices topped with roasted and spiced red bell peppers, Zucchini, onions and creamy avocado slices. This fresh jicama tortillas recipe is just what you need to liven up your taco night! Unlike other low carb tortilla recipes, this one is 100% veggies. And as great as low carb flours are, let’s face it – jicama nutrition content is higher than any low carb flour based tortillas. Plus, the slight crunch and subtle sweetness in jicama tacos actually works perfectly with Mexican flavors.

Jicama Taco

Prep time: 15 mins    Cook time: 15 mins    Total time: 30 mins    Yields: 4 tacos

Ingredients

  • Thinly sliced jicama (I bought mine ready from Tader Joes
  • Sliced Avocado
  • 1 small yellow onion, sliced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp parsley
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ cup chopped green onions or chives
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. Mix the oil, balsamic, sugar, parsley, salt and pepper in a bowl and whisk
  2. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Toss the bell peppers, Zucchini and onions with enough olive oil mix to lightly coat it. Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a large baking sheet and roast until softened, about 20 to 15 minutes.
  3. Lay out the jicama slices and layer it with the roasted vegetables and avocado.
  4. Top it with fresh cilantro.
