Naan (No Yeast)


Indian flatbreads are an essential part of the indian day to day meal. It is generally made with wheat flour, but can also be made with plain flour or maida which is known as naan bread. One such simple and easy variation is the homemade naan made with tawa or pan in the cooking stove. Traditionally and in restaurants, naan is made in the tandoor. Tandoor oven is loaded with coal heat and the naan bread is stuck to the sides of tandoor till it gets baked. The charcoal would infuse the smoky flavour to the bread. This recipe used baking powder instead of yeast. It is important to let the dough rest for a minimum of 2 hours.

Naan (No Yeast)

Prep time: 15 mins    Cook time: 20 mins    Resting time: 2 hrs    Total time:2 hrs 35 mins    Yields: 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 cup plain flour
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • ¼ cup yogurt
  • water, for kneading
  • 1 tsp garlic (optional)

Garlic Butter for topping (optional)

  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp garlic, grated
  • 2 tbsp coriander, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl take 2 cup plain flour, ¼ tsp baking soda and 1 tsp baking powder and ¾ tsp salt. mix well
  2. Add 2 tbsp oil, ¼ cup curd and 1 tsp garlic (optional).
  3. Mix well making sure everything is well combined. Add water as required and knead to a smooth and soft dough.
  4. Grease the dough with oil, cover and rest for 2 hours.
  5. Prepare garlic butter by mixing 3 tbsp butter, 1 tsp garlic and 2 tbsp coriander.
  6. After 2 hours, knead the dough slightly to remove any air present in the dough. Pinch a ball sized dough.
  7. Gently roll to oval shape using a rolling pin. Make sure you do not make it too thin nor thick, 1/4 inch thick. And also keep the size in mind. It should not be larger than your tawa/pan size.
  8. Grease with water over naan. Make sure you coat uniformly. this helps naan to stick on tawa/pan.
  9. Slowly flip over and put it on hot tawa/pan. Remember, be gentle and put the water coated side down to tawa/pan. Also do not use nonstick tawa/pan.
  10. Slightly press down. This helps the naan to stick on to the tawa/pan and when you flip the tawa/pan upside down it will be intact.
  11. After a minute, flip the tawa/pan upside down and cook naan directly on flame till they turn golden brown.
  12. Brush with some prepared garlic butter mixed with coriander leaves, if using or just plain butter
  13. Gently scrape the naan from the bottom and remove.
  14. Finally, serve garlic naan hot with your favourite curry like palak paneer.
